Getting there

Madrid is a wonderful city with very modern public transit. If you choose to stay in the city then you can get most of the way by taking Line 9 of the metro to Arganda del Rey. However, since we're getting married in a "casa rural" or rural bed and breakfast it's admittedly a bit out of the way. Once you get to Arganda, you can take the 327 bus to the center of Tielmes which will leave you ten minutes away walking. Alternately, Spain has both Uber and Taxis which you can hail from either Arganda for a reduced fare, or all the way from Madrid as we're only about 30 minutes away from the city center.


Finally, if neither a cab nor Uber are up your alley and you simply want to explore more of the Spanish countryside, then renting a car is a great option!

If taking this final choice you'll be here in no time and we have plenty of parking. We've mapped out where to park for you.

  • In the blue zone there are more spaces and greater availability
  • The green zone has more traffic but you can park anywhere in that area.

    • A final note: Please leave spaces closest to the venue for those with reduced mobility or above the age of 65.

    What is cocktail attire? We asked the internet so you don’t have to:

    Cocktail attire is the sweet spot between “fancy” and “please don’t make me wear a tux.” It’s perfect for daytime weddings or outdoor events where you want to look sharp and still breathe.
